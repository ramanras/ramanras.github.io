Research

With rising global energy demand and increasing CO₂ emissions, there is an urgent need to develop efficient energy conversion and storage technologies. My research bridges fundamental electrochemistry with practical applications by leveraging multiphysics modeling to optimize advanced electrochemical systems — fuel cells, metal-air batteries, and CO₂ utilization devices — through integrated computational simulations and experimental validation.

My doctoral work focuses on the multiphysics modeling of electrochemical CO₂ utilization in metal-CO₂ systems (Mg-CO₂ and Zn-CO₂) for simultaneous H₂ production and metal bicarbonate synthesis, integrating multiphase flow dynamics, mass transport phenomena, and electrochemical kinetics.

⚗️

Metal-CO₂ Electrochemical Systems

Modeling Mg-CO₂ and Zn-CO₂ primary batteries for simultaneous H₂ production, power generation, and CO₂ utilization via multiphysics transient models.

🌊

Multiphase Flow & Bubble Transport

Euler–Euler bubble flow dynamics in electrolyzers and metal-air batteries; studying gas-phase distribution and its effect on electrochemical performance.

💻

Multiphysics Modeling & Simulation

3D/2D transient COMSOL models integrating dilute solution theory, porous electrode theory, Darcy's Law, and electrochemical kinetics validated against experimental data.

♻️

CCUS Technologies

Developing scalable CO₂ capture, conversion, and utilization technologies addressing critical challenges in carbon neutrality and renewable energy integration.
